Key Supporting Examples

Some suffered as a result of the program
  • Braceros faced discrimination and segregation, and their compensation was delayed.
  • Their children lacked appropriate child labor protections.
  • Many agribusiness workers faced poor working conditions, while having greater difficulty with unionizing.
  • Chicanos faced the threat of deportation.
People in power benefitted from the program
  • Agribusiness leaders had an expendable, cheap labor force that couldn't unionize.
  • Members of government and the press defended the program for financial and political support.
Social structures changed in response to the program
  • Mexican government policymaking moved towards a greater focus on migration and remittances for wealth generation.
  • The Bracero Program was a rallying point for the rise of the Chicano Movement.
